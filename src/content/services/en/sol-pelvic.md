## What is the Pelvic Floor?

The pelvic floor is a set of muscles and tissues that close the lower part of the pelvis and support organs such as the bladder, uterus and rectum. It has essential functions such as sphincter control, visceral support and sexual function.

## Most Common Problems

Pelvic floor physiotherapy is dedicated to helping you in cases of:

### Urinary Incontinence
- Urine leakage during effort (coughing, laughing, jumping)
- Urinary urgency
- Nocturnal leakage

### Prolapses
- Sensation of weight or falling at pelvic level
- Uterine, bladder or rectal prolapses

### Postpartum
- Rectus abdominis diastasis
- Perineal muscle weakness
- Scar pain (cesarean, episiotomy)

### Pelvic Pain
- Pain during sexual intercourse (dyspareunia)
- Tailbone pain (coccydynia)
- Chronic pelvic pain
